Excitement and nervousness in Romania
As I stepped off the plane in Bucharest, Romania, I felt a mix of excitement and nervousness. This was my first time in Eastern Europe, and I wasn't sure what to expect. But as I made my way through the airport and out onto the streets of the city, I quickly began to feel at ease.

The first thing that struck me about Romania was its stunning architecture. From the Gothic-style buildings of Brasov to the art nouveau designs of Bucharest's Old Town, every corner seemed to be adorned with beautiful and unique buildings. I found myself constantly stopping to take photos and marvel at the intricate details of each structure.

As I traveled around the country, I discovered that Romania has so much more to offer than just its architecture. The natural beauty of the Carpathian Mountains took my breath away, and I spent hours hiking through the forests and valleys. I also visited the painted monasteries of Bucovina, which are unlike anything I've ever seen before. The vibrant colors and intricate designs of the frescoes left me in awe.

But what really made my trip to Romania special was the people. I found Romanians to be incredibly welcoming and friendly, always eager to share their culture and history with me. Whether I was enjoying a traditional meal of sarmale and mici at a local restaurant or chatting with locals in a village, I felt a real connection to the people of Romania.

Of course, traveling in Romania isn't always easy. There were times when I struggled to communicate due to the language barrier, and the country's infrastructure can be a bit rough around the edges. But for me, those challenges only added to the adventure.

Overall, I would highly recommend traveling to Romania to anyone who loves culture, history, and natural beauty. It's a country that has so much to offer, and I can't wait to go back and explore more.
